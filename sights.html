About Sights Itinerary
Golden Gate Bridge:
  • Located in Marin County
  • When built, it spanned 4,200 feet and was named longest suspension bridge in the world until 1964
  • Leads you towards the Golden Gate National Recreation Area, Discovery Bay Museum, Muir Woods National Monument, and more

    • Painted Ladies:
  • Located around Alamo Square
  • One of the most photographed locations in San Francisco
  • They have been used in over 70 films and TV shows, including Full House

    • Palace of Fine Arts:
  • Located in the Marina District
  • Was built for the 1915 World Fair
  • The venue is 17 acres

    • Coit Tower:
  • Located in the Telegraph Hill neighborhood
  • Offers panoramic views over the city and bay
  • The tower is 210 feet (64 m) tall

    • Lombard Street:
  • Located one block south of Russian Hill Park
  • Famous for a steep, one-block section with eight hairpin turns, making it the most crooked street in the world

    • Alcatraz Island:
  • Located in San Francisco Bay, 1.25 miles offshore from San Francisco
  • Opened to the public for tours in 1973 (closed prison in 1963 because it was too expensive to continue operating)
  • 36 men tried 14 separate escapes and nearly all were caught or didn't survive the attempt, except three particular inmates: Click here to learn more about their escape

    • Transamerica Pyramid:
  • Located in the Financial District
  • It is the second tallest building in San Francisco, standing 48 stories tall
  • Unique shape was built for environmental practicality in order to let natural light and airflow reach the crowded streets

    • Chinatown:
  • Located on Grant Avenue and Stockton Street (in the Chinatown District)
  • Largest Chinatown outside of Asia - it covers 24 city blocks
  • Oldest Chinatown in North America - established in 1848 as a result of a large boom of Chinese immigrants coming to the U.S.

    • Ferry Building:
  • Located in the Embarcadero District
  • Inside is a food hall lined up with restaurants
  • First opened in 1898, when it was the largest project ever undertaken in the city

    • Twin Peaks:
  • Located in San Bernardino County
  • Offers 360-degree views of San Francisco and the Bay Area
  • Named "Twin Peaks" for a pair of 922-foot-high summits
  • The North and South Twin Peaks, also known as "Eureka" and "Noe" are about 660 ft (200 m) apart

    • Bay Bridge:
  • Process of building was actually three bridges into one
  • The bridge was never given an official name, it goes by San Francisco-Oakland Bay Bridge (or just Bay Bridge)
  • Approximately 260,000 vehicles travel across this bridge everyday

    • Cable Car:
  • One of the few places in the world where people can ride on cable cars
  • Back then, cable cars serviced the entire city but now, there are only three lines remaining
  • The price to ride one way for everyone, regardless if you're a child or an adult, is $8

    • Golden Gate Park:
  • Large urban park consisting of 1,017 acres of public grounds, which is 20% larger than New York's Central Park
  • The very first tweet was made in Golden Gate Park in front of its herd of bison
  • With 24 million visitors annually, Golden Gate is the third most-visited city park in the United States after Central Park and the Lincoln Memorial

    • Fisherman's Wharf:
  • Most popular for Pier 39, including waterfront restaurants and a sea lion colony lying on wooden docks that were originally used for docking boats
  • In 1985, the wharf was used as a filming location in the James Bond film A View to a Kill

    • The Wave Organ:
  • 25 organ wave-activated acoustic pipes
  • Sound is created by the impact of waves against the pipe ends and the subsequent movement of the water in and out of the pipes
  • Best sounds during high tide: Click here to check the tides
