About Sights Itinerary
Marina Bay Sands:
  • Distinctive pool on rooftop overlooks the city and measures 478 feet in length, making it the world's largest rooftop infinity pool
  • Houses 2,561 room hotel
  • Consists of three 55-story towers and infinity pool looks like a ship afloat

    • Gardens by the Bay:
  • The park consists of three waterfront gardens: Bay South Garden, Bay East Garden and Bay Central Garden
  • There is a light show every night of the week at the Supertree Grove from 7:45-8:45pm, featuring dancing lights and music

    • Merlion:
  • Located near the Central Business District
  • It is a combination of two creatures: head of a lion and a body of a fish
  • The statue is 28 ft tall

    • Singapore Flyer:
  • Able to see up to 28 miles away ‚Äì 2 miles more than the entire length of Singapore island city
  • From the Flyer, you are able to see Changi Airport, Sentosa Island, and even parts of Malaysia and Indonesia
  • World‚Äôs largest observation wheel (outside of the United States)

    • HSBC Rain Vortex:
  • Located inside the Jewel Changi Airport
  • World's largest and tallest indoor waterfall (131 ft tall)
  • Waterfall design was inspired by Singapore's tropical rains

    • Helix Bridge:
  • Located outside of The Shoppes at Marina Bay Sands
  • Walking bridge, there are no vehicles
  • The unique design was inspired by the structure of human DNA (colored letter base pairs are lit up on the bridge)
  • The bridge is 919 ft long

    • ArtScience Museum:
  • Located outside of The Shoppes at Marina Bay Sands
  • Opened in February 2011
  • Exhibitions that explore aspects of science including big data, particle physics, paleontology, marine biology, cosmology, and space exploration

    • The Shoppes:
  • Over 300 shops featuring various luxury brands
  • World‚Äôs only floating Louis Vuitton Island Maison
  • There is an Apple store entirely surrounded by water

    • Orchard Road:
  • Located in central Singapore
  • The road runs 2.2 km, or 1.4 miles, and is known for its numerous upscale shopping malls and restaurants
  • The name came from the orchards and plantations that existed in the area until the turn of the 20th century

    • Sky Garden:
  • Located on the 51st floor of CapitaSpring
  • Offers stunning views of Singapore, including Marina Bay Sands
  • In addition to the views, there is a rooftop garden
  • Entry is free (however, you still have to make reservations ahead of time)

    • Spectra Light Show:
  • Located on the water just outside of The Shoppes at Marina Bay Sands
  • Free nightly outdoor 15-minute show that includes fountain jets, colourful visual projections, lasers, lava and mist effects, and an orchestra soundtrack
  • Show was launched in 2017 and tells the story of Singapore‚Äôs journey

    • Sentosa Island:
  • Known for beaches, luxury hotels, and attractions (such as Universal Studios Singapore and Resorts World)
  • One way to get to the island is through the Singapore Cable Car which is a gondola lift, providing an aerial link from Mount Faber on Singapore Island to Sentosa across the Keppel Harbour

    • Fort Canning Park:
  • Located in the southeast portion of Singapore, within the central business district
  • Entry into the park is free but visitors can pay $7.50 for a guided tour
  • The park features many botanical gardens, gallerys, parks, and more that showcases the area's rich history

    • Chinatown:
  • Traditional foods at vendors include Hainanese chicken rice, noodles and satay
  • Visit the many popular Hawker Centres, open-air food courts, that serve a diverse range of foods
  • Most vendors specialize in a specific dish and perfect it over generations, some of them have even earned Michelin stars

    • Clarke Quay:
  • Located along the Singapore River
  • Boat tours are available that cruise up and down the length of the river
  • Known for restaurants and lively night life
  • JUMBO Seafood Restaurant, located in Clarke Quay, is well known for their chili crab
