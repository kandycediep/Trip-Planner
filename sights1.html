Colosseum:
  • Located in Rome
  • Built in 80 AD
  • Once used for gladiatorial games
  • Largest ancient ampitheater ever to be built (and largest standing amphitheatre in the world)

    • Trevi Fountain:
  • Located in Rome
  • Built in 1762
  • Legend says that a coin thrown into the fountain will ensure another return to Rome
  • The fountain stands around 85 feet (26 meters) tall and is 160 feet (49 meters) wide

    • Pompeii Site:
  • Located in Pompei
  • Roman city was buried under ash after the eruption of Mt. Vesuvius in 79 AD
  • Originally remained undiscovered after eruption for 1,500 years
  • Around 2,000 people died during the eruption

    • Amalfi Coast:
  • Popular cities within Amalfi Coast include: Ravello, Positano, Amalfi, and Sorrento
  • Famous for seaside towns, cliffs, and beaches
  • Known for scenic hikes and views

    • Verona:
  • Located in Northern Italy
  • Famous for being the setting of Shakespeare‚Äôs "Romeo and Juliet"
  • The city is known for its Roman antiquities

    • Tuscany:
  • Region located in Central Italy (capital is Florence which is known for its Renaissance art and architecture)
  • Best known for landscapes (vineyards) and food/drink (wine)

    • Capri:
  • Located just off the coast of Naples
  • Known for being the home of the "Blue Grotto," a sea cave that creates a blue reflection, illuminating itself inside
  • Faraglioni's are most associated with Capri (stacks of rocks and some have a central cavity, large enough to allow for the passage of a small boat)

    • Venice:
  • Located in Northern Italy
  • There are no roads in Venice, only canals
  • Popular sights include: the Grand Canal, Rialto Bridge, and Piazza San Marco
  • The city is sinking and studies show that it may be underwater as early as 2100 if global warming keeps accelerating

    • Lake Garda:
  • Located in Northern Italy
  • The area of the lake is approximately 370 square kilometers (143 square miles), making it Italy's largest lake
  • Glaciers formed the alpine region at the end of the last ice age

    • The Dolomites:
  • Located in mountain range in northeastern Italy
  • An abundant amount of scenic hikes and lakes
  • They get the nickname of "Pale Mountains" because of their rock color

    • Puglia:
  • Located in Italy's southern region
  • Popular cities to visit: Bari, Ostuni, and Polignano a Mare
  • Known for stunning coastlines and picturesque whitewashed hill towns

    • Sardinia:
  • Large Italian island located in the Mediterranean Sea
  • Around 1,243 miles (2,000 km) of coastline, sandy beaches, and mountainous interior with hiking trails
  • Known for beaches, turquoise sea, and rock formations

    • Cinque Terre:
  • Located in Northwest Italy
  • Known for collection of coastal villages
  • Liguria (region located within Cinque Terre) is best known for the birthplace of pesto sauce

    • Duomo di Milano:
  • Second-largest cathedral in the world
  • The architectural style is a combination of Gothic and Renaissance
  • The height of the cathedral stands at 108 m (354 ft)
  • It took around 600 years for the cathedral to be completed
