About Sights Itinerary

San Francisco is most famous for the Golden Gate Bridge, its steep streets, delicious variety of cuisines, Alcatraz, and much more! It is the 13th largest city in the United States and is located in Northern California (part of the Bay Area), 6 hours from Los Angeles. The population of San Francisco is 883,255 with a growth rate of 0.10% yearly. The city stretches out to around 47 square miles, surronded by the San Francisco Bay on the east, and the Pacific Ocean towards the west, connected by the Golden Gate strait, which is spanned by the Golden Gate Bridge. There are around 36 neighborhoods with the most popular ones being Fisherman's Wharf, Marina, North Beach, Richmond, and Union Square.


In San Francisco, the summers are long, comfortable, arid, and mostly clear while the winters are short, cold, wet, and partly cloudy. Over the course of the year, the temperature typically varies from 45¬∞F to 72¬∞F and is rarely below 39¬∞F or above 83¬∞F. The most characteristic feature of the weather, however, is the summer fog, which lies low over the city until midday. This fog is a phenomenon of temperature contrasts, created when warm, moist ocean air comes in contact with cold water welling up from the ocean bottom along the coast. The climate around the city is described as a Mediterranean climate, having moist mild winters and dry summers. San Francisco is one of the most windiest places in California, if not THE windiest. The average wind speed is 10.5 MPH, which is moderately average. However, its stronger winds result in an annual maximum wind speed of 92 MPH.
